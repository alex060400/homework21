How do I start producing music?

1. Choosing a DAW

A DAW or Digital Audio Workstation is software that allows for music production and recording. Using a DAW enables artists and engineers to record, track, synthesize, edit, and compose music on a personal computer or studio.

2. Selecting a Midi Controller

The second most important step twoards digital music production is selecting a midi controller. Midi controllers are electronic pianos or drum machines, however when connected to a DAW allows for the user to physically play and record sounds. After connecting a controller to a computer, open a DAW and load in a virtual instrument. From there you should be able to play and control a variety of synthesizers, effects, and instruments all from one single controller.

3. Selecting VSTs

In order to play, record, and synthesize music one must have intruments/effects, however In digital music production there are almost limitless possibilities with VSTs or Virtual Studio Technology. VSTs are essentially digital versions of instruments and effects allowing users to attain expressive or distinct sounds while on a completely digital basis. Popular VSTs can range from pianos, wavetables, and amplifiers to highly meticulous audio synthesizers and samplers. Using VSTs one can even achive almost impossible or never before heard sounds, often out of the limits of normal studio hardware and instruments.
