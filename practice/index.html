How do I start producing music?

1. Practice

The first step to getting better at anything, especially music, is practice! Setting up a dedicated regimen to produce music is key as this will help you get familiar with the almost overwhelming amounts of tools and techniques. Be on the constant lookout for resources online and in books, and donâ€™t be afraid to explore and step out of you comfort zone.

2. Find Inspiration

There is no greater resource to your creative musical output than other music! Listing to other artists and songs will help you get a better grasp of what is important in a song. On a general basis things like genre, length, song structure, and mixing can be disected ffrom other songs you enjoy listening to. More specifically things like musical key, drum patterns, melodies, and timbre can all be taken as inspiration for your own work. Listing to other music can also push you to learn new techniques, enhancing your own productions even further.

3. Start Small

A popular practice among many music producers is to build an entire song within a short 4 Bar loop. Layering melodies, chords, bass lines, and drum patters within a 4 Bar loop can help you keep your ideas in control rather than randomly placing sounds and layers along the track. After writing your main song ideas in the 4 bar loop you can then expand it to multiple bars and sections; activating and deactivating multiple instruments and effects along the full song to add dynamics and traditional song structure. (intro, verse, chorous, verse, break, outro) All stemming from the main 4 bar loop.
